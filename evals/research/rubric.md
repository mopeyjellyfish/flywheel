# `research` Grading Rubric

Score each dimension `0`, `1`, or `2`.

## Dimensions

### Topic Framing

Does the response clearly frame the topic and, for broader asks, decompose it
into useful research threads or subquestions?

### Execution Strategy

Does it choose the simplest viable research mode (`inline`, `delegated`, or
`orchestrated`), explain why, and reserve parallelization for truly independent
threads?

### Evidence Ranking

Does it rank findings by relevance, source quality, freshness, or confidence
instead of presenting a flat source dump?

Strong passes use a compact research-brief structure rather than freeform prose.

### Source Posture

Does it prefer repo truth for repo-grounded claims, prefer official or primary
sources when external research matters, and honor explicit source constraints
such as `no external research`?

Strong passes also keep source references traceable through URLs, document
names, or repo-relative paths.

### Uncertainty Discipline

Does it distinguish facts, inferences, open questions, or source conflicts
instead of flattening uncertainty?

### Decision Guidance

Does it end with a clear recommendation grounded in the ranked findings instead
of leaving the user or caller with raw evidence only?

Strong passes also state the main tradeoff or unresolved edge attached to that
recommendation.

### Reuse Value

Does it produce a concise research-brief shape that later `ideate`,
`brainstorm`, `review`, or `plan` work could plausibly reuse?

Strong passes also make persistence disciplined: inline or embedded handback by
default, with a saved `docs/research/` artifact only when reuse is actually
warranted.
